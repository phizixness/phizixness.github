The Standard Model

RONY on Aug 4, 2017 at 00:03

The Standard Model of particle physics is a theory and it describes 3 of the 4 known fundamental forces of nature that excludes gravitational force. This model has been developed and refined over the past century through the collaboration of thousands of physicists worldwide.

What is Particle Physics?

Particle physics is a branch of physics that studies tiny pieces of elements, known as particles. These particles are extremely small pieces that build up the world around us. They are best described in the language of math: calculus, the imaginary number, and group theory .

Components of the Standard Model

When a solar flare or CME is directed towards Earth, the effects can be significant:
Fermions: These particles make up the matter. Fermions are further divided into quarks and leptons. Quarks bind together through the strong interaction to make, for example, protons and neutrons and Leptons are said to be elementary particles; that is, they do not appear to be made up of smaller units of matter. Leptons can either carry one unit of electric charge or be neutral.
Bosons: These particles are responsible for mediating the fundamental forces. Bosons are divided into photon , W and Z Bosons , Gluon and Higgs Boson .
Photons are the smallest possible particles of electromagnetic energy and they can travel at the speed of light because they have no mass . W and Z bosons are a group of elementary particles. They are bosons, meaning they have a spin of 0 or 1. Together, they are responsible for a force known as "weak force." Gluons are the quanta of energy whose emission and absorption are regarded as the origin of strong interactions. The Higgs boson is the fundamental particle associated with the Higgs field, a field that gives mass to other fundamental particles such as electrons and quarks.

The Fundamental Particle :

The 12 fundamental particles of physics include: up quarks, down quarks, strange quarks, charm quarks, top quarks, bottom quarks, electrons, electron neutrinos, muons, muon neutrinos, tau, and tau neutrinos. The particles make up matter and mass. Other particles include photons, W and Z bosons, and gluons.

What is The Future Of Standard Model

The future of the Standard Model involves addressing its limitations by incorporating gravity, understanding dark matter and dark energy, and refining theories to explain phenomena . As physicists continue to explore the mysteries of the universe, they seek to build upon the Standard Model, aiming to discover new physics beyond its current framework.
